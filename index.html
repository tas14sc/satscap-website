The Thesis

Led by Bitcoin - the Digital Asset space came roaring out of the gates only to fail to deliver on the hype. The interesting thing about hype is that it is often not wrong, just early. This is exactly where crypto sits today - out of the public eye with sentiment levels towards the space at unprecedented lows. Here is the key - the entire space has been quietly building in the silence and preparing for a large revival.

The emergence of AI is commoditizing software. Anything centralized and permissioned can be replicated faster and cheaper than the incumbent ever built it. Crypto is software too, which at first glance looks like a death sentence. It isn't. The market misses the chasm between centralized software and decentralized systems. AI can rebuild QuickBooks or Salesforce at a fraction of the cost. It cannot bootstrap decentralization, recreate Bitcoin's mining network, or replicate Hyperliquid's liquidity. Those are moats that even superintelligence cannot abstract away. Crypto is not an app to be copied. It is infrastructure that has to be coordinated, and coordination at scale is the one thing software alone can't manufacture.

For most of its history, crypto demand was speculative. That is changing. Three forces are now converting it into structural demand. Individuals continue turning to digital assets as a hedge against the steady debasement of fiat currency. Institutions have moved from the sidelines to allocation, with the ETFs marking the very early stages of that shift. And now AI agents have arrived as the first users the rails were actually built for, transacting at machine speed across borders with no human in the loop. Each driver pulls demand from a different direction. Agentic commerce is the newest and the most consequential, because it is the first time a category of user requires crypto rails rather than merely choosing them. Demand built on necessity behaves differently than demand built on speculation. It is the foundation of durable value.

I read something about AI and crypto the other day that broke my brain in a way that I cannot unthink....

Crypto was never for us.
