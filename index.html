The Thesis

As AI agents move from novelty to necessity - booking, buying, hiring, transacting on our behalf - they hit a wall. The legacy financial system requires human authorization, institutional intermediaries, and geographic boundaries. It was never built for autonomous agents operating at machine speed.

A rapidly growing slice of this activity cannot run on the existing system - not because legacy rails are broken, but because they were never built for non-human participants. That requires different rails. It requires crypto.

The bug becomes the feature. Structural demand replaces speculation.
